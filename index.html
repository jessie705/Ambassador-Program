Who ambassadors are

Culture Carriers

Not pace leaders. They are trusted, consistent members who model our values and make people feel welcomed and connected.

Consistent Presence

Show up most weeks. Arrive early, linger after. Be visibly approachable. That alone changes how new runners experience the group.

Community Builders

Spark conversations, celebrate milestones, and pull people toward post-run hangouts. Help turn a run into a community.

Honest Voices

Share real feedback through quarterly surveys. Tell us what's working, what's not, and what newcomers are experiencing.

Core expectations

01

Welcome newcomers

If you see someone standing alone before the run starts, that's your cue. Don't wait for them to approach.

02

Be present & approachable

It's not just about attendance. It's about being the kind of person a nervous first-timer would feel okay walking up to.

03

Model the culture

Pace conversations can unintentionally exclude people. Redirect toward effort, enjoyment, and experience instead of times and splits.

04

Build post-run energy

Attend post-run hangouts occasionally. Help normalize gathering together instead of dispersing the moment the run ends.

What you receive

🍕

🏃

Stepping back

Circumstances change. If you need to step back, we just ask for an early conversation so we can plan ahead.
